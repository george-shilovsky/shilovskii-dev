Privacy policy

This Privacy Policy explains how the Migaja application ("Migaja", "we", "us", "our") handles information when you use it on your Mac. We are Georgii Shilovskii, an individual developer, contactable at support@shilovskii.dev.

If you do not agree with this Privacy Policy, please do not install or use Migaja.

Summary

Migaja does not collect, transmit, or share any of your data. Everything you copy, search, or configure stays on your Mac. There is no account, no telemetry, no cloud sync, no advertising, and no third-party analytics. The semantic-search neural model is bundled with the app and runs entirely on-device. Apple processes your subscription payment through the App Store under Apple's own terms.

If you ever want to delete everything, use Settings → History → Reset all clips inside the app.

1. What information we handle, and where it lives

When you use Migaja, the app handles the following information locally on your Mac only:

Data Where it lives Why
The text of items you copy to the clipboard (excluding password-manager and concealed-pasteboard items) A local SQLite database inside the app's sandbox container on your Mac (open it via Settings → History → Reveal database in Finder) So you can search and recall them later.
The bundle ID and display name of the app from which each item was copied Same SQLite file To show the source-app icon next to each clip.
A numeric vector representing the meaning of each text item (an "embedding") Same SQLite file To power semantic search.
Your settings (retention, hotkeys, exclusion list, theme, etc.) macOS UserDefaults for the app To remember your preferences.
Your free-trial start timestamp macOS Keychain (not iCloud-synchronised) To enforce the 7-day trial.

None of this data is uploaded, transmitted to a server we control, or shared with any third party. We do not see it, and we cannot recover it for you if you lose it.

2. What information we collect

None. We do not collect personal data, usage data, diagnostic data, advertising identifiers, or analytics events. Migaja does not include any third-party SDK for tracking, attribution, crash reporting, or analytics.

3. How we process your information

We do not process any of your information, because we do not receive any of it. All processing — capturing clips, computing embeddings, running search — happens on your Mac, against the local SQLite database described in Section 1.

4. When and with whom we share information

We do not share any of your information, because we do not collect any.

The only party involved in your use of Migaja besides you and us is Apple Inc., which processes your subscription payment through the App Store under Apple's Privacy Policy. Migaja receives only the resulting entitlement signal ("you have an active subscription") — no payment-card numbers, billing addresses, or Apple-account identifiers.

5. Cookies and tracking technologies

Migaja is a native macOS application. It does not use cookies, web beacons, pixels, or any other web-tracking technology, and it does not host or load any web content from us or from third parties for tracking purposes.

6. International data transfers

Migaja has no servers. Your data does not leave your Mac, so no international transfer of personal data occurs as part of using the app.

The only network activity initiated by Migaja is the StoreKit traffic handled by macOS and the App Store for subscriptions. That traffic flows directly between your Mac and Apple's infrastructure under Apple's own terms.

7. How long we keep your information

Because all data is stored locally, retention is controlled by you, from inside the app:

  • Each clip is kept on your Mac until it is older than your configured Retention period (Settings → History → Retention period; default 90 days, options 30 / 90 / 365 days / Unlimited).
  • When the configured Maximum clips cap is exceeded (Settings → History → Maximum clips; default 10,000, options 1k / 10k / 100k / Unlimited), the oldest unpinned clips are soft-deleted.
  • Soft-deleted clips disappear from search results immediately and are physically purged from the database within 24 hours.
  • Pinned clips are exempt from both the retention period and the maximum-clips cap.
  • Settings → History → Reset all clips permanently deletes every clip and reclaims disk space (PRAGMA vacuum).

To erase everything while keeping the app installed, use Settings → History → Reset all clips. Uninstalling the application removes the app from your Mac; run Reset all clips first to be sure no clipboard history remains.

8. Information from minors

Migaja is not directed at children. The app is rated 4+ in the App Store only because it contains no age-restricted content; it is not intended for children's use.

We do not knowingly collect or process information from anyone — adult or minor — because, as described above, we do not collect any information at all. If you believe a child has come to use Migaja and you would like guidance on how to delete the local clipboard history from their Mac, contact us at support@shilovskii.dev and we will help.

9. Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live (e.g. under the EU/UK GDPR, California's CCPA/CPRA, Canada's PIPEDA, South Africa's POPIA, or Australia's and New Zealand's Privacy Acts), applicable privacy law may grant you rights to access, correct, delete, port, or restrict processing of your personal information, and to withdraw consent.

Because all of your data stays on your Mac and we never receive it, those rights are exercised entirely by you, on your own device:

  • Right of access. Open Settings → History → Reveal database in Finder to inspect the SQLite database that holds your clips. You can open it with any standard SQLite client.
  • Right to rectification. Edit or replace any clip by re-copying its replacement, and delete the original from the popup.
  • Right to erasure. Soft-delete individual clips from the popup (purged within 24 hours), or use Settings → History → Reset all clips to delete everything immediately.
  • Right to data portability. The database is a standard SQLite file — copy it anywhere.
  • Right to withdraw consent. Use Settings → History → Reset all clips to erase your data, then delete the app.
  • Right to object / restrict processing. Quit Migaja from the menu-bar icon to stop all clipboard capture immediately. Reopen when you want it active again.

If your jurisdiction's law grants you a formal data-subject-access right against us and you wish to invoke it in writing, email support@shilovskii.dev. We will respond within the timeframe required by the applicable law. Note that we will only be able to confirm that we do not hold any data about you, since we do not collect any.

We have not appointed a Data Protection Officer, as the scope of our processing (none) does not require one under the GDPR.

10. On-device artificial intelligence

Migaja's "semantic search" feature is powered by a neural model called multilingual-e5-base (Q8 quantised, ~289 MB, MIT-licensed). The model is bundled inside the app at the time of download. It is not fetched from the internet at runtime, and no part of it communicates with any server.

All inference runs on your Mac using the open-source llama.cpp library, accelerated by Apple's Metal framework. The text of your clips is converted into numeric vectors on-device. The model auto-unloads from memory about 30 seconds after the last search to free RAM.

Nothing is sent to OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Apple Intelligence, or any other AI service. Semantic search works fully offline.

11. Sensitive data we deliberately avoid

Some applications mark their pasteboard contents as transient or concealed — password managers in particular do this. Migaja honours those signals and does not store any pasteboard entry marked with the standard transient (org.nspasteboard.TransientType), concealed (org.nspasteboard.ConcealedType), or remote-clipboard (com.apple.is-remote-clipboard) flags.

In addition, Migaja ships with a default app-exclusion list — 1Password, Bitwarden, Keychain Access — and lets you add more from Settings → Exclusions. Clipboard items copied from excluded apps are not stored at all.

If a sensitive item still makes it into the history, you can soft-delete it (it disappears immediately and is purged within 24 hours), or hard-reset the entire database from Settings → History → Reset all clips.

12. Permissions Migaja may ask for

None. Migaja does not request Accessibility, Full Disk Access, Automation, Screen Recording, Contacts, Calendar, Photos, Microphone, Camera, Location, or any other macOS permission. When you select a clip, Migaja writes the text to the system clipboard via the public NSPasteboard API (no permission required) and gives focus back to the app you came from — you press ⌘V yourself. Migaja never simulates keystrokes and cannot read content from other apps.

13. Third parties

  • Apple Inc. processes your subscription payment through the App Store under Apple's Privacy Policy.
  • The app embeds open-source components — GRDB.swift (MIT), llama.cpp (MIT), ggml (MIT), and Apple's Accelerate and Metal frameworks. These components run locally as part of the application; they do not communicate with the internet.

No other third party receives any data from Migaja.

14. Do-Not-Track signals

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature you can activate to signal a preference not to be tracked online. Because Migaja is a native macOS application that performs no online tracking of any kind, DNT signals are not applicable. There is nothing for us to track, with or without a DNT signal.
